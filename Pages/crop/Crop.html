Crops

Wheat

A cereal grain that produces a fine white flour that is mostly used in breads, baked goods (such as cakes and crackers), and pastas (such as macaroni or spaghetti), as well as animal feeds.

Rice

The starchy seeds of an annual Southeast Asian cereal grass (Oryza sativa) roasted and eaten. This cereal grass is frequently grown in hot regions for its seeds and byproducts.

Barley

A cereal grass (genus Hordeum, particularly H. vulgare) with flowers in thick spikes with long awns and three spikelets at each rachis joint. Its seed is mostly utilised in malt drinks, breakfast meals, and livestock feed.

Sugar Cane

A tall, robust perennial grass native to tropical Southeast Asia with a big terminal panicle that's also frequently grown in warm climates as a source of sugar.

Cotton

A soft, generally white fibrous material made up of the hairs that envelop the seeds of numerous upright, freely branching tropical plants of the mallow family (genus Gossypium).

Corn

The grain of a cereal grass that is a region's principal crop (such as wheat in Britain and oats in Scotland and Ireland).

Soyabean

Glycine max is a hairy annual Asian legume that is commonly planted for its oil-rich proteinaceous seeds as well as fodder for soil improvement.

Coffee

Any of numerous Old World tropical plants (genus Coffea, particularly C. arabica and C. canephora) of the madder family that are commonly farmed in warm places for their coffee-producing seeds.

Potato

An erect South American nightshade plant (Solanum tuberosum) extensively grown for its tasty starchy tuber.
